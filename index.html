Pre Business

In Chapter 4, we focused on the concept of creating a business and the different aspects one should consider before starting a business. There are many aspects that come with starting up a business, but we decided to focus on mergers and acquisitions. A merger is the combining of two corporations or other business entities to form one business and acquisition which is similar but on a larger scale regarding larger corporations. In order for a corporation to pay for an acquisition, they would have to use a leveraged buyout, that uses borrowed money to fund the payment. There are three types of mergers: horizontal, a merger between firms that make and sell similar products or services in similar markets; vertical, a merger between firms that operate at different but related levels in the production and marketing of a product; and a conglomerate, a merger between firms in completely different industries. We believe that this concept is important because it shows one way for smaller businesses to grow through mergers as well as prevent these firms from going bankrupt.

In this chapter, we were introduced to the concept of franchising and its advantages. A franchise is a license that is purchased to operate and own an individual business, as if it were part of a chain of stores. The actual granting of a franchise is conducted between a franchisor (individual or organization that grants the franchise), and a franchisee (person purchasing the franchise). Overall, we believe that this concept is a key takeaway because franchising has allowed companies and businesses to grow steadily, and franchises have proven to be more successful than independently owned small businesses. Franchising has also been a way for present companies to continuously grow as they provide the materials needed to help the franchisee run the business smoothly and according to the franchisor’s standards.

In chapter 5, we learned about the advantages and disadvantages of owning a small business. Some of these advantages include a growing relationship with customers and employees, the ability to adapt to change, simplified record keeping, independence, profit retention, and the ability to keep business information private. Disadvantages that can harm a small business are the risks of failure, limited potential, and a limited ability to sufficiently raise capital. Our group found this concept interesting because it is essential to take these factors into consideration when deciding to start a new business.
